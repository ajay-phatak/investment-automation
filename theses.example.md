# Investment Theses

## Strategy Context

I run a thesis-driven, asymmetric-upside book. I look for situations where the
market's consensus pricing implies a probability distribution I disagree with —
either underpricing tail risk on a structural shift, or overpricing the
near-term path of something I think is more durable than the tape suggests.

Preferences:
- ETFs for thematic / sector exposure where stock-picking risk is high relative to thesis risk.
- Single names only when the catalyst is concentrated (specific regulatory decision, earnings setup, binary event).
- Comfortable with multi-quarter holding periods if the catalyst path is intact.
- Macro hedges welcome when they're uncorrelated with the rest of the book, not just directional.

Suggested tickers should reflect this: prefer broad/liquid ETFs for index exposure, and where suggesting single names, lean toward ones with clear catalyst geometry (earnings dates, regulatory milestones, FDA decisions, etc.) rather than generic large-cap exposure to a theme.

## Thesis: AI infrastructure spending will exceed consensus through 2027

The market is treating hyperscaler AI capex as a cyclical bulge that normalizes once frontier-model training scaling hits diminishing returns. I think it's structural and consensus is too low because (a) inference workloads scale with deployed agents, not with model size, and (b) every enterprise rolling out internal copilots is now a perpetual buyer of compute and observability, not a one-time training customer. Picks-and-shovels names with consumption-based revenue should re-rate as 2026 prints come in above the implied trajectory.

## Thesis: US cannabis rescheduling lands in 2026, sooner than the market is pricing

Current MSO equity prices appear to assume a 12-18 month timeline for DEA Schedule III rescheduling, with significant probability of further delay or reversal. I think the timeline is shorter because the December 2025 Trump executive order on rescheduling combined with the April 2026 psychedelics EO signals a coordinated regulatory pivot, and the Senate has already removed blocking language from the relevant spending bill. Schedule III eliminates 280E tax burden, which is a step-function increase in MSO free cash flow — not a multiple expansion story, an earnings story.

## Thesis: Offshore wind in the US is permanently impaired this cycle, but global wind is not

The market is treating "wind" as a single trade and is dragging global wind names down with US offshore wind sentiment. I think this conflates two very different stories: US offshore wind is structurally impaired under the current administration (permitting freezes, federal lease pauses), but onshore wind in Europe and emerging markets continues to scale on independent policy drivers. Globally diversified wind exposure should be cheap relative to the actual cash-flow trajectory of the underlying utilities and developers, with the US offshore overhang as the sentiment anchor.
